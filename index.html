Classes and Services

All our coaches are certified by the lean startup company and Kromatic in tactics and tools that have a proven record of success. For more information on these classes and services, please click an icon below.

  • Introduction To Innovation

    • This 2-day course has been broken down into more digestible 2.5 hour blocks over four days for the virtual environment.

    Learn how to use the key tools and strategies for understanding your customers, mapping out your learning, communicating your idea and running experiments to get you to your next great idea! Or even just to just help you think a little differently about how you operate today.

    Learn how to use the same tools and tactics as startups like AirBnB and Uber to get your project or idea off the ground in the government environment.

  • Herding Cats 101

    • As a manager or supervisor, how do you balance the demand for leadership for innovation with the need accomplish today's mission? How do you include "failed" projects and "learning" on your annual evaluation and hope to be recognized? How can you be a more effective advocate for your teams and their work? How do you hold your teams accountable for their learning when there's no clear metric for success?

    In this class, we'll cover the foundational i-corps principles, strategies for managing innovation projects, how to measure success, and learning to balance today's mission with our visions for the future. Here you'll learn how you can create an environment that fosters innovation, new ideas, and discovery while still ensuring today's mission gets done.

  • Mapping Your Project

    • What are all the moving parts necessary for a business or idea to succeed? This workshop will teach you how to map it all out. Think of it as a one-page business plan for your idea and program. Tell the story of you project through clear, simple language that communicates who's involved, what value you're bringing to the table, and how you'll know when you've succeeded

  • Creating Your Vision

    • It's hard to create great solutions without first understanding the problem. It's even harder to solve the problem when you don't have a vision of the end-state in mind. We're here to help you with both!
    During this two hour workshop we will discuss:
    - The components of a good problem statement
    - What makes for a bad problem statement
    - The difference between a vision, mission, and goal
    - The key components to each
    - What makes having this vision so key to the success of your project or idea

  • Testing Your Ideas
    • Have you ever worked on something that didn't get used?
    Have you ever spent weeks or months working on an idea only to find out it was completely wrong?
    What if you had tested your idea in a small way first?

    This workshop is designed to teach you a methodical approach to reducing uncertainty, buying down risk, and learning more quickly which ideas to pursue and which ones to let die. We'll give you a framework and several tools to help you identify critical assumptions, plan experiments that will give you the certainty you're after, and give you the confidence to make decisions based on data and evidence.

  • How To Measure Success

    • Success is a difficult thing to measure when you're not dealing in dollar signs and bottom lines. How do you know when things are "better"? What are you going to measure? How are you going to make it consistent? Here, we'll teach you how to find the metrics that will work for you and help you define "success" for you and your project! "What gets measured gets improved" and we'll show you how!

  • Getting to Know Your Customers

    • How do you know if you're delivering value to your customers? Do you even know who your customers are?? This workshop is designed to teach you how to get the information you need so you can make decisions confidently based on information from the people who matter the most, your customer! How should you talk to them? What can you learn about them? How can they provide you with the direction you need in your project.
